# Go-To-Market — The Systematic Approach

> How the vision (`../vision/vision.md`) actually reaches people: internet → YouTube → owned community → in-person workshops → scale.
> Read this as a sequence. Don't skip phases — each one is the soil for the next.

---

## The core operating principle

**You are the product, and reps are the only path.** Every figure in this space won by publishing *consistently, in their own voice, for years.* The strategy below is just a system for maximizing the quality and compounding of those reps. No phase works without the founder showing up on camera and in the room.

The funnel in one line:

> **Short-form (discovery) → Long-form (trust) → Email/App (ownership) → Workshops (transformation) → Programs/Retreats/Certification (scale).**

---

## Phase 0 — Foundation (Weeks 1–8, before you go loud)

Get these locked *before* mass posting. Launching without them wastes your best early reps.

1. **Pick the name** (vision §8) and secure handles everywhere + domain. Do this in one day; don't let it block for months.
2. **Write the message architecture** — one page: the belief, the enemy ("you are broken" culture), the promise, the signature framework (the 5 Fires), and 10 core "hooks" you'll repeat forever.
3. **Define the signature framework** and commit to its fixed container (vision §6).
4. **Build the minimum kit** — see `resources-and-investment.md` §Lean. You need: a phone/camera that shoots clean, a mic, decent light, and an editor (yourself at first, then outsourced fast).
5. **Do the practice on yourself, on camera, privately, for 4 weeks.** You cannot sell a state change you aren't visibly living. This is content R&D and integrity insurance simultaneously.
6. **Set the cadence you can sustain for 12 months** (see Phase 1). Pick a rhythm you won't break — consistency beats volume.

**Exit criteria:** name live, 1-page message doc done, 20 content ideas in a backlog, first 4 weeks of practice logged.

## Phase 1 — The Content Engine (Months 1–12): build the face

This is the internet/YouTube machine. The model that works in this niche:

**One long piece → many short pieces.** Each week you create one "hero" asset and slice it into 5–15 short clips.

### The weekly production loop
- **1 long-form** per week: a 10–25 min YouTube video *or* a podcast episode (you teaching, you practicing live, or you in conversation). This is the depth/trust layer.
- **5–7 shorts** per week, sliced from the long-form + shot natively. This is the discovery layer. Shorts are where "expression of life" lives — high energy, 30–60s, one idea, one feeling.
- **3–5 written posts** per week (X / Instagram captions / LinkedIn if relevant). Use the existing `tweet-forge` skill and your style profile for these.

### What to actually make (content pillars — rotate them)
| Pillar | Example | Funnel job |
|---|---|---|
| **The practice, demonstrated** | "Do this 2-min breath with me right now" | Show, don't tell. Instant felt result. |
| **The reframe / provocation** | "Stop trying to heal. You were never broken." | Stops the scroll, plants the belief. |
| **The devotional case study** | "The impossible situation Krishna faced — and the move he made" | Depth + story + ownable angle nobody else does. |
| **The celebration / life** | You dancing, training, living it out loud | Sells the *vibe* — people buy the life, then the method. |
| **The teardown** | "Why most self-help keeps you stuck" | Positions against the field, defines the enemy. |

### Decision rules
- **Hooks are 80% of the work.** First 3 seconds decide everything. Write 5 hook options per piece, ship the best.
- **Ship before perfect.** A live, raw, energetic piece beats a polished dead one. (Vision: "boring" is the only real failure.)
- **One platform native, then repurpose.** Don't try to be everywhere natively. Win YouTube + one short-form platform; cross-post the rest.
- **Watch saves/shares, not likes.** Shares = "this expressed me." That's the movement signal.

### Metrics / gates (don't move to Phase 2 until ~Month 6–9)
- Posting cadence held for 6 months straight (the real test).
- A few pieces that broke out (10x your median views).
- First 5,000–10,000 followers and a *clear* "this is the one that works" format.

## Phase 2 — Own the Audience (Months 6–18): turn viewers into a list

Followers are rented; an email list and an app/community are owned. This is where audience → students.

1. **Lead magnet:** a free, genuinely transformational mini-experience — e.g. *"The 7-Day God Mode Reset"* (a short daily practice from the 5 Fires, delivered by email/app). This is the single highest-leverage asset; it converts viewers into a relationship.
2. **Email list + a free community** (start with a Discord or Skool/Circle; graduate to your own app later). Weekly value, behind-the-scenes, live calls.
3. **First paid product — keep it small and proven:** a low-priced (~$50–200) self-paced program or a paid challenge built on the 5 Fires. Validate that people *pay and complete and transform.* Don't build the big thing yet.

### Decision rule
- **Don't build the app/platform until a manual version sells.** Sell the challenge over email + Zoom first. Build software only once demand is proven (this is the same "validate before you build" discipline as your product work).

### Gate to Phase 3
- 1,000+ email subscribers, first paid cohort completed with documented before/after stories.

---

## Phase 3 — In-Person: the irreplaceable layer (Months 12–30)

This is the Tony Robbins / Joe Dispenza moat — and the antidote to AI commoditizing everything online. A felt, collective, in-the-room state change cannot be downloaded. It's also the deepest loyalty and the highest revenue.

**Ladder up — never start with the big event:**

1. **Local small workshops (10–30 people).** Cheap venue (yoga studio, event space). Run the 5 Fires live. *This is your live R&D* — you'll learn more here than from 100 videos. Charge modestly ($50–150).
2. **City workshops / day intensives (50–150 people).** Once small ones sell out and reviews are strong. Add production: music, lighting, a real arc to the day.
3. **Flagship weekend event (200–1,000+).** The "Unleash"/"Week-Long" equivalent. Multi-day, high production, the full transformation arc. This becomes the signature pilgrimage.
4. **Retreats (15–40 people, premium).** High-touch, high-margin, deep transformation. Beautiful location. The top of the value ladder.

### Decision rules
- **Sell out small before scaling up.** An undersold big event kills momentum and money. Demand-first, always.
- **Production is the differentiator.** The vision says "not boring" — in-person, that means lighting, sound, music, pacing, ritual. Budget for it (see resources doc §Events).
- **Safety is non-negotiable** for breathwork/intense practices — trained facilitators, screening, medical awareness, consent. (Also a legal necessity — resources doc §Risk.)
- **Capture everything.** Every live event is also a month of content. Film it.

### Gate to Phase 4
- Repeatable sold-out workshops, strong NPS, a waitlist forming.

## Phase 4 — Scale & the Culture Layer (Year 2–4+)

Now you turn a person into a movement and an income into an enterprise.

1. **Signature paid programs / flagship course** — the deep, premium online program (the 5 Fires mastery). Higher price ($500–2,000), cohort-based for community + completion.
2. **Certification / train-the-trainer** — license your method to certified facilitators (Dispenza has 200+; this is how you scale beyond your own body and reach cities you'll never personally visit). Major revenue + reach multiplier *and* a cult-risk mitigant (decentralizes you).
3. **The app** — daily practice, the 5 Fires, community, live drops. Recurring revenue, daily habit, owned distribution.
4. **The culture layer (the hip-hop identity made real):** music drops, merch, collabs with artists, live "experiences" that feel like concerts. This is what makes it a *movement people wear and play*, not a course they bought. This is your Kanye/hip-hop differentiator — lean into it hard once the foundation is real.
5. **Books / big stages / podcasts circuit** — go on every big podcast (the Aubrey Marcus / Lewis Howes / London Real circuit), write the book that codifies the philosophy. This is how you cross from "known online" to "well-known, period."

## The whole sequence at a glance

| Phase | Timeline | Goal | Don't move on until… |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 — Foundation | Wk 1–8 | Identity + kit + practice | Name live, message doc done, 4 wks practice |
| 1 — Content engine | Mo 1–12 | Build the face | 6 mo consistent, format that works, 5–10k followers |
| 2 — Own audience | Mo 6–18 | Viewers → students | 1k+ list, first paid cohort with proof |
| 3 — In-person | Mo 12–30 | Irreplaceable transformation | Repeatable sold-out workshops |
| 4 — Scale + culture | Yr 2–4+ | Person → movement | (ongoing) |

> Phases overlap on purpose. Start owning the audience (2) while the content engine (1) is still ramping. Start small workshops (3) the moment you have 1,000 true fans — don't wait for a million followers.

## Distribution truths to internalize (from the competitors)

- **Eric Thomas:** built on *one* viral format ("How Bad Do You Want It"), raw delivery, audience-involving, vulnerable. → Find your one format and hammer it.
- **Prince Ea:** 20M+ followers on short films + spoken word; now teaches a "Creator Journey" accelerator. → Short-form virality is a *learnable system*, and the audience itself becomes a product later.
- **Joe Dispenza:** workshops + certified trainers = scale beyond his body. → Live events and certification are where the real money and reach are.
- **Aubrey Marcus:** podcast + fellowship + product empire, sole-sponsored Joe Rogan early. → A single deep relationship/bet can outperform broad spray; the podcast is the trust engine.
- **Tony Robbins:** live state-change at scale + relentless business rigor. → The room is the moat; treat events as a craft and a business, not an afterthought.
