When comparing Manish's notes against course transcripts, do NOT frame output as
"what you missed" / coverage gaps. His note-taking style is intentional: capture
the reusable trick, contrast, and his own synthesis — not transcribe the lecture.

**Why:** His style *is him* and matters more to him than coverage. A missed-content
list misreads the notes as incomplete transcription when they're a deliberate
second brain on top of the transcript.

**How to apply:** Maintain the single file `second-brain/style-profile.md` (do NOT
create new style files — this is the canonical one). It already characterizes his
style in depth; add iterative refinements to its "Small refinements he's open to"
section. Keep suggestions tiny, additive, one-at-a-time; never a backlog dump, never
"rewrite." Add new refinements incrementally as more lectures are reviewed.
